6. Likely dead ends

The following are probably not worth pushing further as pure hand arguments:

Pure category slack alone.
The slack-4 layer allows interior zero-excess splits. Categories with both zero-domination excesses positive can hide non-tight zeros.

Trying to prove ZD is too large or too small by scalar inequalities only.
The zero-rectangle cuts are structural. Without the actual missing singleton core F, ZD bounds are too weak.

Treating all locally dominated vertices as C-tight.
This is invalid. C-tight follows from equality, e.g.

δ+α=2orδ+β=2,

not from ≥2.

Jumping directly to fixed-P.
